“ARTIFICIAL WOMB”--- A place where humans can be grown entirely from scratch!

Shubham Das

INTRODUCTION


The mastery of in vitro fertilisation and improvements in the survival of premature infants have opened new opportunities for ectogenesis, the implantation and full development of foetuses in vitro.Here, ectogenesis denotes the availability of an artificial uterus capable of fostering the development ofembryos to viability.An artificial uterus could assist women with dam-aged or diseased uteri by allowing them to conceiveand carry infants, despite an inability to do so ontheir own. A second possibility is that an artificialuterus could serve as an incubator for preterm babies, specifically those who are delivered before approximately 24 weeks of gestation—the minimumfor viability with current incubators. The development of such an incubator could provide a break-through for reducing fatal mortality and morbidity that stems from prematurity.“Artificial wombs,” as they are currently conceived, would function by connecting to an extra-corporeal supply of maternal blood or replacementfluids. The artificial womb would supply nutrientsand oxygen to an incubated foetus and would be capable of disposing of waste materials. This would,therefore, necessitate an artificial placenta for mediating the necessary exchanges between fatal circulation and the system that would replace the maternalflow.The Artificial Womb facility; a place where humans could be grown entirely from the scratch! The devices within the Artificial Womb Technology is called “Growth Pods”. Each Growth Pod is designed to replicate the same conditions that exists inside the mother’s uterus. Growth Pods are designed to host human foetuses until they are fully developed. The Artificial Womb are designed to help premature babies to continue developing after their birth.

But emerging research is making it positive to use them to create designed humans. Every human foetus required around 37weeks inside the mother’s uterus to fully develop and then comes birth. But not every baby has this biological luxury. Infact every year around 15 million foetuses are born too early and approximately 1 million of them die due to birth complications. The minimum number of works in which a developing foetus must stay in the womb is 24weeks, if the foetus is born before that the survival rates will go down. And even the foetus survive it leads to lung issues and long-term neurological damage.

Working of Artificial Womb?


In terms of design the artificial womb consists of: the growth chamber, which hosts the foetus. It replicates the same environment provided by the mother’s uterus. It is the incubation chambers that provides the optimal temperature and humidity for the growth of the foetus. There is another container which provides the foetus with the constant stream of blood that is rich in oxygen until the moment of birth. The Artificial Womb is filled with amniotic fluid, which is the liquid that surrounds the foetus inside the mother’s uterus. This liquid is rich of the essential nutrients that are needed to sustain the unborn foetus inside the womb. The Growth Chamber also features advanced sensors compelled with Artificial Intelligence. These sensors monitor the foetus vital signs during the development process which includes breathing and heartbeat.

The Artificial Womb also features a screen which displays red-tire data on the development progress of the foetus. Inside this growth pod, the foetus is kept for 9 months until a full course of development is reached. The idea of growing babies inside an artificial environment is called “Ectogenesis”. Ecto meaning outside, and genesis means the formation. It isn’t really new. The history of the artificial womb dates to the early 1950’s.

The first design of artificial womb was planted by Emanuel Greenberg back in 1955. He developed the concept with the hope of helping premature babies. In present time, the real artificial womb is no more than just a plastic bag filled with amniotic fluid. It doesn’t look like much, but it serves its purpose: allowing premature foetuses to continue developing outside the mother’s uterus. Even though the primary/principal goal of an artificial womb is to sustain premature babies. It could actually be used to create the perfect human being.

By engineering a human being from scratch we can redesign ourselves to be more resistant to diseases and to achieve more intelligence and better looks and babies who are born by the above mentioned process are known as “Designer Babies” or “Designer Humans”.
